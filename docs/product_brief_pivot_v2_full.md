# Revised Product Brief — Module-First Pivot

## Status
This document reframes the product based on the latest commercial direction discussed internally.

The app should no longer be positioned primarily as a **care-specific platform**.  
Instead, it should be positioned as a **modular HR operations and case management platform** with optional **sector-specific packs**, starting with care.

This is a **product packaging and go-to-market pivot**, not a technical reset.

---

## 1. Core strategic shift

### Previous direction
The app was being repositioned as a:
- social care workforce management platform
- care-specific HR and workforce operations product
- vertically focused care solution

### New direction
The app should now be positioned as a:
- modular HR operations platform
- case management and manager workflow system
- configurable module-based product
- platform with sector-specific overlays/packs

### Strategic summary
**Build a broad modular HR workflow platform first.  
Launch care as the first specialist sector pack, not the whole product identity.**

---

## 2. Why this pivot is stronger

### Commercial advantages
This approach:
- broadens the addressable market beyond care
- makes packaging and pricing easier
- allows Ellipse to sell into multiple client types
- avoids boxing the product into one vertical too early
- still preserves care as the strongest specialist entry point

### Product advantages
This approach makes the product easier to explain:
- core platform
- standard modules
- optional add-on modules
- specialist sector packs

That is more commercially flexible than making the whole product care-specific from the outset.

---

## 3. New product positioning

### Recommended primary positioning
A modular HR operations and case management platform for employers, with optional sector-specific packs.

### Stronger commercial version
A modular HR workflow platform combining probation, supervision, performance, employee relations workflows, AI guidance, and advisor escalation, with specialist packs for care and other sectors.

### Ellipse-friendly version
A practical HR workflow platform that gives clients structured modules for people processes, with optional sector-specific packs and direct escalation into expert advisor support.

---

## 4. Core product model

The product should now be designed in four layers.

## Layer 1 — Core Platform
The shared base used by all clients.

### Includes
- authentication and permissions
- organisation setup
- users and access levels
- employee/people records
- dashboards and workspace shell
- timeline/history tracking
- documents and file storage
- template rendering/generation
- AI warning/consent framework
- advisor escalation workflow
- reporting framework
- API-readiness foundation

### Purpose
Provides the shared operating system for the platform.

---

## Layer 2 — Standard Modules
These are the practical workflow modules that can be bundled as standard.

### Recommended standard modules
- probation
- supervision
- PIP / capability

### Why these first
These are broad enough to apply across many organisations and give the platform an immediate practical use case.

---

## Layer 3 — Add-On Modules
These are optional additional workflow areas that can be sold separately or enabled by tier.

### Recommended add-on modules
- investigation
- disciplinary
- grievance
- sickness / absence
- return-to-work
- compliance
- reporting / insights
- onboarding workflows
- document packs
- AI workflow support

### Purpose
Creates commercial flexibility and allows clients to expand over time.

---

## Layer 4 — Sector Packs
These are sector-specific overlays or packs.

### First sector pack
**Care Pack**

### What sector packs should influence
- terminology
- templates
- workflow defaults
- recommended process steps
- reporting widgets
- compliance extras
- dashboard language
- example content
- module defaults

### Strategic role
Sector packs should sit on top of the modular platform, not define the whole platform itself.

---

## 5. First vertical pack: Care

Care should still be the first specialist pack because it is the strongest market Ellipse understands operationally.

### Care Pack could include
- care-specific templates
- social care terminology
- service/home/branch structures
- safer recruitment and onboarding checklists
- compliance tracking extras
- supervision/welfare patterns aligned to care settings
- care-oriented dashboard metrics
- investigation/disciplinary flows common in care environments

### Positioning rule
Care remains the best first wedge, but it should be described as the **first specialist configuration**, not the whole product.

---

## 6. Differentiator: AI guidance + advisor escalation

This email thread highlights a particularly strong commercial differentiator.

### The differentiator
The product should not just be:
- HR workflow software
- AI HR assistant software

It should be:
- structured workflow software
- AI guidance with explicit warnings/disclaimers
- escalation into Ellipse advisors when needed

### Core proposition
Users can:
1. structure the issue in the platform
2. receive AI-generated guidance with warnings
3. accept that AI is guidance only
4. escalate the case to an Ellipse advisor
5. send relevant history/documents as part of the escalation

### Why this matters
This creates a hybrid commercial proposition:
- software efficiency
- human advisory reassurance
- clearer risk management
- stronger differentiation from generic SaaS tools

## 8. Naming implications

This pivot makes broad, people/process-oriented names stronger than care-specific ones.

### Stronger naming direction
Names like **PeopleHarbour** fit this strategy better because they suggest:
- people operations
- guidance and support
- a broader category than care only
- compatibility with modular packaging
- a strong fit for “AI guidance + advisor escalation”

### Weaker naming direction
Care-specific names are now less suitable as master product names because they make later expansion harder.

## 11. Strategic summary

The strongest next move is not to abandon the current work, but to **reframe it**.

### Final recommended statement
**The app should become a modular HR workflow and case management platform with optional sector-specific packs, starting with care, and differentiated by AI guidance plus direct advisor escalation.**

That keeps:
- the current technical base
- the best existing module work
- the care market advantage
- the future opportunity to sell more broadly
